The Planets of Our Solar System

Welcome to our cosmic neighborhood the Solar System.

The Planets

Earth

Our home planet, the only known world with life. Earth’s surface is covered by 70% water and has the perfect balance of atmosphere and temperature to sustain living organisms.

The Moon

The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite and our closest neighbor in space. It has no air or water, but its gravity controls Earth’s tides and keeps our planet’s rotation stable.In 1969, humans first walked on its surface during the Apollo 11 mission.

The Sun

The Sun is the center of our Solar System and the source of all light and energy on Earth. It’s a massive sphere of hot plasma whose gravity holds the planets in orbit and sustains life on our planet.

Neptune

The most distant planet from the Sun.Neptune is a deep blue world with the strongest winds in the Solar System — reaching speeds over 2,000 km/h!

Jupiter

The largest planet in our Solar System a giant made mostly of gas.Jupiter’s Great Red Spot is a massive storm that has been raging for centuries. Its strong magnetic field and dozens of moons make it a fascinating world of extremes.

Venus

Venus is often called Earth’s “twin” because of its size and structure, but it’s far from friendly. Its thick atmosphere traps heat, making it the hottest planet in the Solar System — even hotter than Mercury.

Mars

Known as the “Red Planet,” Mars has captivated scientists for centuries. With its vast deserts, towering volcanoes, and signs of ancient riverbeds, it remains humanity’s top candidate for future colonization.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its spectacular rings made mostly of ice and dust. It’s a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, with more than 140 moons orbiting it.

Uranus

Uranus is a unique planet because it spins on its side, possibly due to a massive collision in its past. Its bluish-green color comes from methane gas in its atmosphere.

Dwarf Planets

Pluto

Once considered the ninth planet, Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006. It remains a symbol of humanity’s curiosity and exploration.

Ceres

The largest object in the asteroid belt and the first dwarf planet to be visited by a spacecraft.

Haumea

Oval-shaped due to its fast rotation, with two moons and a bright icy surface.

Makemake

Eris

Slightly smaller than Pluto but more massive. One of the farthest known objects orbiting our Sun.
